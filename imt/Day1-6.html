What a day! Let's review what we've accomplished so far:

Completed:

  • Sprint 1: Create the project and app.

  • Sprint 3: Create the login/logout dynamics

For another day:

I feel pretty proud and accomplished! I definitely think the blog by Chris Bartos helped set my perspective well. It's best to tackle learning Django (or anything) by putting yourself slightly outside of your comfort zone. Also the availability of tutorials and classes online is great, but following them directly can be a crutch. It was a great experience using the tutorials to extrapolate what to do with the app I am creating. I'm honestly suprised, yet quite pleased that everything worked out so well.

Confusions Left

I still am trying to wrap my head around routing urls to the app urls.py file and where templates need to be created. Because I was piecing together my project from multiple tutorials I ended up putting my templates in various locations and they all seemed to work. Maybe Django is just robust enough

I think the biggest uncertainty is how the forms are populated for Login and Signup. I think that Django just knows what to put there based off their location, HTML name, and objects. One of the tutorials mentioned changing the signup interface being doable but outside of the scope of the tutorial. We'll see if I get there.
